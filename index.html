About the High Sierra Trail

The Route

Built by the Civilian Conservation Corps and completed in 1932, the HST runs roughly 72 miles from the giant sequoias of Crescent Meadow to the summit of Mt. Whitney — the highest point in the contiguous United States at 14,505 ft.

The trail makes two major climbs: Kaweah Gap (10,700 ft), crossing the Great Western Divide, and Trail Crest near the very end, before joining the John Muir & Pacific Crest Trails for the final push to the summit.

Along the way it passes Hamilton Lake, the granite bowl of Precipice Lake, the vast Kern River Canyon, and Kern Hot Spring — a natural soak at the river's edge that's a favorite rest stop for thru-hikers.
